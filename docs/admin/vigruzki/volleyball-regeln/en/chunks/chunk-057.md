15.9 ILLEGAL SUBSTITUTION

15.9.1 A substitution is illegal if it exceeds the limits specified in Rule 15.6
(except in the case of Rule 15.7), or if an unregistered player is
involved.

15.9.2 If a team has made an illegal substitution and play has resumed, the
following procedure applies, in order:

15.9.2.1 the team is penalized with a point and the next service for the
opponent;

15.9.2.2 the substitution must be corrected;

15.9.2.3 all points scored by the faulty team from the moment of the fault are
cancelled; the opponent’s points remain valid.

15.10 SUBSTITUTION PROCEDURE

15.10.1 The substitution must be carried out within the substitution zone.

15.10.2 A substitution lasts only the time necessary for recording the
substitution on the score sheet and for the players to enter and leave
the court.

15.10.3.1 The actual substitution request begins when the substitute player(s)
arrive(s) ready to play in the substitution zone during an interruption.
The coach does not need to give a hand signal for the substitution,
except when the substitution is due to an injury or before the start of
the set.

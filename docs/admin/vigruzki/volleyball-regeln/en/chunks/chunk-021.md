# Volleyball Rules

1.2 PLAYING SURFACE

CHAPTER 1
FACILITIES AND EQUIPMENT

The playing surface includes the playing court and the free zone. It
must be rectangular and symmetrical.

The playing court is a rectangle measuring 18 x 9 m, surrounded by a
free zone at least 3 m wide on all sides.

The free playing space is the space above the playing surface that is
free of any obstacles. The height of the free playing space is at least
7 m from the playing surface.

For FIVB, world and official competitions, the free zone must extend
5 m from the sidelines and 6.5 m from the end lines. The free
playing space must be at least 12.5 m high, measured from the
playing surface.

1.2.1 The surface must be flat, horizontal and uniform. It must not
pose a risk of injury to the players. Playing on rough or slippery
surfaces is forbidden.

For FIVB, world and official competitions, only a wooden or synthetic
surface is allowed. Any surface must be previously approved by the
FIVB.

1.2.2 For indoor courts, the surface of the playing court must be a
light colour.

For FIVB, world and official competitions, white colours are
required for the lines. Other, contrasting colours are required for
the playing court and the free zone. The court may be executed in
different colours to distinguish the front and back zones.

1.2.3 For outdoor courts, a slope of 5 mm per metre is allowed for
drainage.

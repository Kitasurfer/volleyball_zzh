3 BALLS

3.1 STANDARDS
3.2 UNIFORMITY OF THE BALLS
3.3 BALL-RETRIEVAL SYSTEM

The ball must be spherical and made of a flexible leather or synthetic
leather cover with a bladder made of rubber or a similar material.

Its colour may be a uniform light colour or a combination of colours.

The synthetic leather material and the colour combinations of balls
used in official international competitions must conform to FIVB
standards.

Its circumference is 65–67 cm and its weight 260–280 g.

Its internal pressure must be 0.30 to 0.325 kg/cm² (4.26 to 4.61 psi)
(294.3 to 318.82 mbar or hPa).

All balls used in a match must have the same standards in terms of
circumference, weight, pressure, type, colour, etc.

FIVB, world and official competitions, as well as national or league
championships, must be played with balls approved by the FIVB,
unless otherwise agreed by the FIVB.

For FIVB, world and official competitions, five balls must be used. In
this case, six ball retrievers are positioned, one at each corner of the
free zone and one behind each referee.

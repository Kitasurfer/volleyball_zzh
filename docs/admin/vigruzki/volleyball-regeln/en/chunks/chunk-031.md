# Volleyball Rules

If the team captain is not on the court, the coach or the team captain
must designate another player on the court to act as game captain.
This game captain retains this responsibility until he/she is substituted,
until the team captain returns, or until the end of the set.

5.2 COACH

When the ball is out of play, only the game captain is entitled to speak
to the referees:

5.1.2.1 to request an explanation of the application or interpretation of the
rules and also to convey the wishes or questions of his/her teammates.
If the game captain is not satisfied with the first referee’s
explanation, he/she may lodge a protest against this decision and
must immediately inform the first referee that he/she reserves the
right to record an official protest on the score sheet at the end of the
match;

5.1.2.2 to request authorization:
  a) to change all or part of the equipment,
  b) to check the positions of the teams,
  c) to check the condition of the floor, net, ball, etc.;

5.1.2.3 in the absence of the coach, unless the team has an assistant coach
who has assumed the coach’s duties, to request time‑outs and
substitutions.

5.1.3 At the end of the match, the team captain:

5.1.3.1 thanks the referees and signs the score sheet to confirm the result;

5.1.3.2 if he/she has informed the first referee in due time, may confirm an
official protest concerning the application or interpretation of the
rules by the referee and have it recorded on the score sheet.

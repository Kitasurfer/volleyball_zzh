# Volleyball Rules

… in which any Libero may participate.

**Interfering action**  
Any action that gives the opponent an advantage, or any action that prevents the opponent from playing the ball.

**O‑2bis**  
An official FIVB form for registering players and team officials. It must be presented at the preliminary inquiry.

**External object**  
An object or person outside the court, or close to the limit of the free playing space, that interferes with the flight of the ball. Examples: overhead lights, the referee’s chair, TV equipment, the scorer’s table, net posts. Antennas are not considered external objects, as they are part of the net.

**Substitution (regular)**  
The action by which a regular player leaves the court and another regular player takes his/her place.

**Team’s first hit**  
There are four situations in which a playing action is considered the team’s first hit:
- reception of the service;
- reception of an opponent’s attack hit;
- playing the ball that rebounds from the team’s own block;
- playing the ball that rebounds from the opponent’s block.

**Protocol**  
The sequence of events before the match, including the coin toss, official warm‑up, team and referee introductions, as described in the Specific Competition Handbook.

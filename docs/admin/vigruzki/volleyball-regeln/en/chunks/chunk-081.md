# Volleyball Rules

28.2.1.1 prepares the Libero control sheet;

28.2.1.2 prepares the reserve score sheet.

28.2.2 During the match, the assistant scorer:

28.2.2.1 records the details of Libero replacements / re‑designations;

28.2.2.2 notifies the referees of any Libero replacement fault using the buzzer;

28.2.2.3 operates the manual scoreboard at the scorer’s table;

28.2.2.4 checks that the scoreboards are consistent;

28.2.2.5 updates the reserve score sheet when necessary and hands it to the scorer.

28.2.3 At the end of the match, the assistant scorer:

28.2.3.1 signs the Libero control sheet and submits it for verification;

28.2.3.2 signs the score sheet.

For FIVB, world and official competitions where an electronic score sheet is used, the assistant scorer works together with the scorer to announce substitutions, direct the second referee to the team requesting an interruption, and identify Libero replacements.

29 LINE JUDGES

29.1 LOCATION

When only two line judges are used, they stand at the corners of the court on the right‑hand side of each referee, diagonally 1 to 2 m from the corner.

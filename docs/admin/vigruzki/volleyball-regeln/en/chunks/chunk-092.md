**Zones**  
These are sections within the playing area (i.e. the court and the free zone) defined for a specific purpose (or with particular restrictions). They include: front zone, service zone, substitution zone, free zone, back zone and Libero replacement zone.

**Areas**  
These are sections of the floor outside the free zone that have a specific function in the Rules. They include, for example, the warm‑up area.

**Lower space**  
This is the space bounded above by the bottom of the net and the cord joining it to the posts, laterally by the posts, and below by the playing surface.

**Crossing space**  
The crossing space is defined by:
- the horizontal band at the top of the net,
- the antennas and their extensions,
- the ceiling.

The ball must pass through the crossing space to reach the opponent’s court.

**External space**  
The external space is located in the vertical plane of the net outside the crossing and lower spaces.

**Substitution zone**  
This is the part of the free zone where substitutions are carried out.

**Unless, with the consent of FIVB**  
This statement recognises that, while there are regulations on standards and specifications for equipment and facilities, FIVB may occasionally make special provisions to promote volleyball or to test new conditions.

**FIVB standards**  
Technical specifications or limits set by FIVB for equipment manufacturers.

**Fault**  
(a) An illegal playing action.
(b) A rule violation that is not a playing action.

24 SECOND REFEREE

24.1 LOCATION

The second referee performs his/her function outside the playing court near the post, on the side opposite to the first referee.

24.2 AUTHORITY

24.2.1 The second referee is the assistant to the first referee but also has his/her own area of jurisdiction.

If the first referee becomes unable to continue his/her duties, the second referee may replace him/her.

24.2.2 He/she may also, without whistling, indicate faults outside his/her jurisdiction, but may not insist that the first referee follow his/her signal.

24.2.3 He/she controls the work of the scorer(s).

24.2.4 He/she supervises the team members on the team bench and reports their misconduct to the first referee.

24.2.5 He/she controls the players in the warm‑up areas.

24.2.6 He/she authorizes regular game interruptions, controls their duration and rejects illegal requests.

24.2.7 He/she controls the number of time‑outs and substitutions of each team and informs the first referee and the respective coach of the second time‑out and of the fifth and sixth substitutions.

24.2.8 In the case of a players injury, he/she authorizes an exceptional substitution or grants a three‑minute recovery period.

24.2.9 He/she controls the condition of the floor, especially in the front zone.

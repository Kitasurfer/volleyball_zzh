# Volleyball Rules

SECTION 2 – THE REFEREES, THEIR DUTIES AND OFFICIAL HAND SIGNALS

30 OFFICIAL SIGNALS

30.1 REFEREES’ HAND SIGNALS

Referees must indicate, by using the official hand signals, the reason for their whistle (the type of fault whistled or the purpose of the authorized interruption). The signal must be held for a moment and, when shown with one hand, the hand must correspond to the side of the team that committed the fault or made the request.

30.2 LINE JUDGES’ FLAG SIGNALS

The line judges must indicate, by using the official flag signals, the type of fault they have signalled and must hold the signal for a moment.

30.3 TECHNICAL OFFICIALS AND EQUIPMENT

The diagrams in this section show the positions of the technical officials (referees, scorers, line judges, ball retrievers, floor wipers) and LED panels around the court, together with the required clear free‑zone distances (for example 20 cm, 15 cm, 5 cm from LED panels; free zone width, etc.).

Other required equipment includes:

- hygrometer
- thermometer
- clock or chronograph
- manual scoreboard
- 2 rubber‑tyred trolleys
- net measuring rod
- six 1‑m‑long mops
- eight absorbent towels
- four red or yellow line‑judge flags (40 x 40 cm)
- manometer
- ball gauges (weight and circumference)
- TV replay light
- one set of post pads
- one spare net
- one pair of spare antennas
- two bibs for Liberos
- bibs for photographers (a different colour for the host broadcaster)
- official jacket for CC members

These specifications apply to FIVB, world and official competitions.

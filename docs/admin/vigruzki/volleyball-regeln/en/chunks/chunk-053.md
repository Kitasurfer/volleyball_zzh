# Volleyball Rules

14.3 BLOCKING WITHIN THE OPPONENT’S SPACE

When blocking, the player may extend his/her hands and arms beyond the net, provided that this action does not interfere with the opponent’s play. It is not permitted to touch the ball beyond the net before the opponent has completed an attack hit.

14.4 BLOCK AND TEAM HITS

14.4.1 A block contact is not counted as a team hit. Consequently, after a block contact the team still has the right to three hits to return the ball.

14.4.2 The first hit after the block may be executed by any player, including the one who touched the ball during the block.

14.5 BLOCKING THE SERVICE

It is forbidden to block the opponent’s service.

14.6 BLOCKING FAULTS

14.6.1 A blocker touches the ball in the opponent’s space before the opponent’s attack hit.

14.6.2 A back‑row player or a Libero completes a block or participates in a completed block.

14.6.3 Blocking the opponent’s service.

14.6.4 The ball is sent "out" from the block.

14.6.5 Blocking the ball in the opponent’s space from outside the antenna.

14.6.6 A Libero attempts an individual or collective block.

In recent years, the FIVB has made great progress in adapting the
game to a modern audience by liberalizing the criteria for ball
handling, introducing up to two specialized defensive players
(Liberos), implementing technology with the Video Challenge System
that fairly evaluates the athletes’ efforts, and promoting a policy that
encourages a flowing game to entertain the audience both in the hall
and on screen.

This text is addressed to a wide volleyball audience – players,
coaches, referees, spectators, commentators and others – because
an understanding of the rules enables better play and personal
satisfaction. Coaches can develop better team structure and tactics
and give players the opportunity to fully develop their skills, and an
understanding of the relationship between the written rules and the
actual actions on the court enables officials to make better decisions.

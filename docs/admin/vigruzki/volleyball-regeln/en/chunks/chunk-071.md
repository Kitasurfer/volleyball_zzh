# Volleyball Rules

CHAPTER 8
OFFICIALS

22 REFEREEING TEAM AND PROCEDURES

22.1 COMPOSITION

The refereeing team for a match consists of the following officials:

- first referee,
- second referee,
- challenge referee,
- reserve referee,
- scorer,
- four (two) line judges.

Their positions are shown in Diagram 10.

For FIVB, world and official competitions, a challenge referee (when VCS is used), a reserve referee and an assistant scorer are mandatory.

22.2 PROCEDURES

22.2.1 Only the first and second referees may whistle during the match:

22.2.1.1 the first referee gives the signal for service, which starts the rally;

22.2.1.2 the first or second referee signals the end of the rally, provided they are sure a fault has been committed and have identified its nature.

22.2.2 They may whistle when the ball is out of play to indicate that they authorize or deny a teamâ€™s request.

22.2.3 Immediately after the whistle that ends the rally, they must indicate with the official hand signals:

22.2.3.1 If the fault is whistled by the first referee, he/she indicates in order:

(a) the team to serve;
(b) the nature of the fault;
(c) the player at fault (if necessary).

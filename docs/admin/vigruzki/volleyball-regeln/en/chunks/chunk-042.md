# Volleyball Rules

9 PLAYING THE BALL

9.1 TEAM HITS
9.2 CHARACTERISTICS OF THE HIT

Each team must play within its own court and playing space (exception:
Rule 10.1.2). However, the ball may be played back from beyond its own
free zone and over the scorer’s table throughout its trajectory.

A hit is any contact with the ball by a player in play.

For returning the ball, a team is entitled to a maximum of three hits (in
addition to the block). If more are used, the team commits the fault of
"FOUR HITS".

9.1.1 SUCCESSIVE CONTACTS

A player may not hit the ball twice in succession (exceptions: Rules
9.2.3, 14.2 and 14.4.2).

9.1.2 SIMULTANEOUS CONTACTS

Two or three players may touch the ball at the same moment.

9.1.2.1 When two (or three) teammates touch the ball simultaneously, this
counts as two (or three) hits (except for a block). If they reach for the
ball but only one of them touches it, one hit is counted. A collision
between players is not a fault.

9.1.2.2 When two opponents hit the ball simultaneously over the net and the
ball remains in play, the team receiving the ball is entitled to three
additional hits. If such a ball goes "out", the fault is on the opponent’s
side.

14.1 BLOCKING

14.1.1 Blocking is the action of players close to the net to intercept the ball coming from the opponent by reaching higher than the top of the net, regardless of the height of contact with the ball. Only frontâ€‘row players may complete a block, and at the moment of ball contact some part of the body must be higher than the top of the net.

14.1.2 BLOCK ATTEMPT
A block attempt is the action of blocking without touching the ball.

14.1.3 COMPLETED BLOCK
A block is completed when the ball is touched by a blocker.

14.1.4 COLLECTIVE BLOCK
A collective block is executed by two or three players close together and is completed when one of them touches the ball.

14.2 BLOCK CONTACT

Successive (quick and continuous) contacts with the ball by one or more blockers are permitted, provided that the contacts occur during a single blocking action.
